1. Information We Collect & Retention

1.1 Personal Data You Provide (retained for the life of your account plus 2 years)

  • Payment Information: We collect payment tokens (credit/debit card tokenized data) via our PCI-compliant provider‚Äîwe do not store raw card numbers; tokens are retained as long as your payment method is active.
  • Communications: Messages you send via contact forms, email, or chat; retained for 1 year.
1.2 Automatically Collected Data (retained for up to 18 months)
  • Usage Data: IP address, browser type, pages viewed, time spent, referrer URLs, and analytics captured via cookies or similar technologies.
  • Device Data: Operating system, device type, and unique device identifiers.
1.3 Third-Party Data (retention per provider policy)
  • Social Login: If you sign up with Google or Facebook, we receive your name, profile picture, and email.
  • Marketing Partners: Demographic or preference data from analytics and advertising partners.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Process Orders & Payments: Validate and fulfill printing requests, including shipping.

3. Cookies & Tracking

3.1 Cookie Categories
  • Essential: Necessary for login sessions and security.
  • Functional: Save preferences (language, themes).
  • Analytics: Google Analytics and similar, for site performance.
  • Marketing: Third-party ads and retargeting.
You can manage or disable cookies via your browser settings; note that this may affect site functionality.
3.2 Third-Party Tracking
We integrate services like Google Analytics and our payment provider, each with its own privacy policies. Please review their terms for details on data handling.

4. Data Sharing & International Transfers

We do not sell or rent your personal data. We may share information with:
  • Service Providers: Shipping carriers, payment processors, cloud hosts, and email delivery services (including cross-border transfers under standard contractual clauses).

  • Business Transfers: In a merger or sale, under confidentiality commitments.

5. Data Security

We use industry-standard measures, including HTTPS encryption and role-based access controls, to protect your data. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is fully secure.
